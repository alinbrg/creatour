France

Human being - a traveller, wanderer, adventurer, explorer of the world… Really, why do people travel, what is an instinct and real deep-seated purpose within the traveller? Why is traveling so intimately exciting, promising and energising? What is truly the sense, expectation and desire that moves travelers to embark on the journey?

Indeed, some of the deepest wirings of what being human means is being curious, wanting to discover more in life and looking for something that is unknown and missing, current and given reality not being enough. Travelling, be it physical, spiritual or intellectual, is the only way of discovering beyond the realms of current existence – and, thus, travelling is the key inspiration to the very nature of human beings.

But, the big question is, how clear really is our purpose when we start to travel and how much are we reaching the purpose? Traditionally people will visit popular iconic places and attractions but does it mean that they have really explored the place before leaving it? Have they understood the place and explored it in the right way? If travel is supposed to be the time of the most exciting and fulfilling experience, really, how is the expectation met in the end? What was the expectation? Has experience acceded to the expectation? Or is the feeling like “yes, but, is this all that there is”? - Surely, it shouldn’t be this way. It is just the same old story of those who follow the crowd and are quickly lost in it.
